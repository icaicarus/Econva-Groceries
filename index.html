What is this site for?


Econva Groceries was created to help find eco-friendly alternatives to traditional grocery shopping, specifically in the Southern Ontario region. Sometimes it can be difficult to know almost every purchase we make, even essential ones, contribute negatively to the environment. There isn't much individual consumers can do to fight the system, except vote with our dollar and support better alternatives to things that destroy our planets ecosystems, which is exactly what our site is for!

What's wrong with traditional grocery stores?


We all know traditional grocery stores use all kinds of plastics that are apparently 'disposable' but can take millions of years to decompose, but what is the true extent of it? Single use plastics contribute to most of the garbage in the oceans, and almost all grocery stores use them. These can be plastic bags, food packaging, and other things that are not made to be reused or last. Historically, things are only getting more and more packaged, as can be seen with cereal boxes, which used to only contain a cardboard box but now also have a plastic bag in them. In just 2018, approximately 30 million tonnes of waste have been dumped into landfills. If you want to know more, check out our about page.
